Main Purpose

A bionic leg is designed to mimic human leg functionality using cutting-edge technology and biomechanics. It not only restores mobility but also ensures user comfort, improving the quality of life for individuals with leg impairments. With its advanced features, users can seamlessly walk, run, and climb stairs with confidence.

Modelling

Method and control system

The computer communicates with the motion control card via the ISA interface to control the DC servo motor and PAMC amplifier, driving hip and ankle movement. The card also regulates the pneumatic artificial muscle's inflation pressure through the pressure proportional valve, influencing knee movement. Movement feedback is relayed to the computer through encoders and potentiometers via D/A.

Limitation and challenges in Modeling

The modified model accurately depicts the muscle's static characteristics under specific conditions. Practical application faces challenges in measuring certain structural parameters, leading to a need for a simpler model. Experiments under constant pressure introduced a fitting function to account for nonlinearity in force and displacement, with defined coefficients.

Optimized Trajectory Tracking in Artificial Leg Design Using Pneumatic Muscles

The paper mentioned an artificial leg design using a four-bar mechanism for the knee, driven by pneumatic artificial muscles. Testing indicated that these muscles mimic biological muscle behavior. A mathematical model was developed and validated with experimental data. Simulations showed the leg can effectively follow a desired path.
